### New sections — woven into the main body (tested equity grounding)
- **§ 8a — Indenture vs. Trust Relation: Public Record vs. Chambers.** New ruleset. The trust *relation* is pleadable in any equity filing; the trust *indenture* (the rules document) is never filed on the public docket. It goes in chambers under seal or stays in the Petitioner's private file. Grounding: classical chancery's protection of trust instruments from public inspection; the in-chambers practice authority already established in v10 § 25.
- **§ 25a — In-Chambers Filing of the Indenture (under seal).** Procedure for tendering the indenture only when the chancellor inquires, by leave, under seal — never via the public docket. Pair with the kingdom book in-chambers petition template.
- **§ 26b — Conflict-of-Variance Notice.** When a respondent or the law-side recasts the equity bill as a contract or consumer-debt matter, the Petitioner files a Notice of Variance pointing to the bill's actual averments. Doctrinal grounding: pleading discipline; "decree justified by pleadings" (Van Zile, Gibson); the Conflict-of-Variance treatise already in `data/legal-treatises/conflict-of-variance-tn-federal.md`.
- **§ 28a — Disgorgement of Securitization Proceeds.** Expansion of v10 § 28 (Four Duties → Remedies). Adds the constructive-trust-ex-maleficio + equitable-tracing prayer mechanics from Victor Chaos's *Bill in Equity for Accounting* (kingdom book companion). Grounding: Pomeroy § 1053, CIGNA v. Amara, Gibson § 954, Beatty v. Guggenheim (Cardozo).
- **§ 34a — Disclaim the Trust Three Ways: Written, Verbal, On-Record.** Reformulated as a doctrinal refusal-rule for *Petitioners* facing officers who attempt to enroll them into an unfavorable trust posture (e.g., probation, plea colloquy, criminal arraignment). Grounding: disclaimer doctrine (Pomeroy § 1086 n., Story § 974), classical equity right to refuse a trustee office; presented as defensive — not as a magic incantation.
- **§ 34b — Misnomer, Idem Sonans, and the Name-Change Predicate.** New section. The Petitioner has obtained a court-issued name-change decree (e.g., *VICTOR CHAOS* → *Victor Chaos*) recorded in the public record. A proceeding captioned *VICTOR CHAOS* is misnamed. The section covers: (1) what the decree must contain and when it must predate the challenged caption; (2) how to file a Notice of Misnomer as a standalone procedural motion under the idem sonans doctrine; (3) how the "show me where my name is" verbal sequence operates *only* as the spoken layer on top of a filed Notice with the decree attached as Exhibit A — never as a standalone verbal move; (4) why the verbal sequence without a decree is always fringe and never the move itself. Grounding: plea in abatement for misnomer, idem sonans doctrine, § 26b Conflict-of-Variance machinery. Pair with kingdom book exhibit A (State Action / 14th Amendment — why personal jurisdiction over the underlying dispute survives even when the caption is misnomered).
- **§ 35a — When the Court Itself Becomes Vacant Trustee.** Where the court or clerk acts as fiduciary over registry funds, bail, fines, fees, bonds — the *Court Accounting* mechanism from Victor Chaos's treatise. Grounding: court registry as private bank; Pomeroy § 1080; bill-in-equity-court-accounting cross-walk.
- **§ 39a — Worked Example (Criminal Module).** Briefer parallel to v10 § 39 worked example. Same trust-by-conduct logic applied to a citation/arraignment posture: traffic stop, ticket, summons. Avoids fringe scripts; sticks to "petition to strike for variance," "bill in equity for accounting of court registry funds," and disclaimer of trustee office.

## 2. Fringe Quarantine List — Appendix Z contents

### Quarantined to Appendix Z

1. **PTLN as ritual/magic identifier.** v10 already treats PTLN as a private ledger crosswalk — that part stays. What gets flagged: any framing that the *number itself* (not the act of capture) carries jurisdictional weight. Source: pt2, pt6.
2. **"You/Your" death-punch script.** Without a predicate name-change decree, this is bare obstruction — courts treat it as sov-cit theater and it damages credibility. It is NOT a magic incantation and it is NOT the move by itself.

   **How it actually works (with the predicate):** The Petitioner obtained a court-issued name-change decree (e.g., *VICTOR CHAOS* → *Victor Chaos*) recorded in the public record. A subsequent proceeding captioned *VICTOR CHAOS* is now *misnamed* — the caption identifies a stranger to the record. The script ("show me where my name is") is the verbal layer on top of a *filed Notice of Misnomer/Variance* with the decree attached as Exhibit A. The clerk is being walked through the Petitioner's own filed record.

   **⚠ Why flagged:** The script is only the top layer. Without the decree, it is pure obstruction and courts treat it accordingly. The name-change decree is the predicate — it is not optional. Any practitioner who runs the script without the decree behind it is doing sov-cit theater, and it will be seen as such.

   **⚠ The verbal-only version is always fringe.** Saying "show me where my name is" in front of a clerk with no filed record behind you is not a plea of misnomer — it is a person wasting the court's time. The transcript material sometimes presents it as if the verbal sequence alone is the move. It is not.

   **Tested-equity grounding:** plea in abatement for misnomer; idem sonans doctrine (names sounding the same are treated as same person *unless rebutted by certified court record*) — the decree rebuts it. § 26b Conflict-of-Variance Notice operationalizes the same principle in writing. § 34b (new, below) is the dedicated main-body treatment.

   *Source: pt7 (verbal script), pt8 (misnomer framing)*

   **New § 34b — Added to main body:** "Misnomer, Idem Sonans, and the Name-Change Predicate." Grounded in plea-in-abatement doctrine, idem sonans, and the petition-to-strike-for-variance procedure. The section establishes: (1) what the name-change decree must contain and when it must predate the caption being challenged; (2) how to file a Notice of Misnomer as a standalone procedural motion; (3) how the "show me where my name is" verbal sequence works *only* as the spoken layer on top of the filed record; (4) why the verbal sequence alone without a decree is always fringe. Pair with kingdom book exhibit A (State Action / 14th Amendment framing for why courts may still have personal jurisdiction over the underlying dispute even when the caption name is misnomered).
3. **Accommodation-party theory** as a private-rights-retention device through qualified endorsement (last-first-middle + grantee front + grantor/beneficiary special-deposit back + SPP).
   *Tested-equity alternative:* § 14 acceptance/declaration in v10 (already does the equity work without the UCC-3 overlay).
4. **$1 stamp tender / folded-dollar-bill / silver-dollar tender as discharge.** Pseudolegal tender folk-practice; no UCC, FRE, or treatise grounding.
   *Tested-equity alternative:* there is none — the equity remedy is accounting and disgorgement, not personal discharge by tender.
5. **Registered-mail-to-self / 3¢-stamp-self-cancellation account.** Treated in transcripts as opening a private equity account. No grounding.
   *Tested-equity alternative:* § 13 the SPP envelope + § 17 three-notice sequence already create the equitable record.
6. **DTC / CEDE & Co. / $2M credit-card securities-account claims.** Specific dollar amounts and account-existence claims sourced from transcripts; not provable on the four corners of any pleading.
   *Tested-equity alternative:* § 28a disgorgement via equitable tracing — pleads "receipts unknown to Petitioner in Respondent's exclusive possession" instead of asserting a specific dollar figure.
7. **Name doctrine (LAST, FIRST MIDDLE; PAC status as gate; BC registration as government training).** Sov-cit / private-American-citizen framework.
   *Tested-equity alternative:* sign as your full legal name. Equity acts on the conscience of the party — not on capitalization.
8. **Treasury-call adverse-attention narrative** as predictive of outcomes. Anecdote, not doctrine.
9. **"Equity takes jurisdiction for one purpose = all things"** as a free-floating maxim. The actual maxim (Russell v. Todd; Story § 72) is narrower — equity having taken jurisdiction "for one purpose" decides every part of *that* controversy. The transcript framing inflates scope.
   *Tested-equity alternative:* v10 § 8 Beacon Theatres + § 11 stage-one/stage-two distinction.
10. **Roark Lesson 9A "merger of titles extinguishes debt by tender of lawful consideration."** The merger doctrine itself is real (Story § 1035 et seq.; treated in pt5); the *tender-of-lawful-consideration trigger* is the fringe overlay.
    *Tested-equity alternative:* § 28a disgorgement + § 7 special-deposit creates the equitable extinguishment posture without the tender-trigger claim.
11. **"In camera demand" as right.** Chambers practice is by leave, not by right (v10 § 25 already states this). Any transcript framing that treats it as demandable goes here.
12. **Three-prong bank challenge** (name on account / exact amount / at-or-near closing time) as a discovery weapon outside of equitable-tracing.
    *Tested-equity alternative:* § 28a equitable accounting + the document-possession averment already in v10 § 9.
13. **JS-44 replacement / private accounting ledger as filing substitute.** v10 already handles JS-44 properly. Any framing that the private ledger *replaces* the cover sheet stays in the appendix.
